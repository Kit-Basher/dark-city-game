Legend

Sphinx, Fallen Angel, Kitsune+

Playing a Legend

You are practically a God, or at least you were when humans used to worship you like one. Around in an era when humanity has decided to ignore magic and pretend the supernatural does not exist. They should worship, but if they found out, who knows what they would do.

Darkest Self

Those that dare try to come between you and what belongs to you will find themselves face to face with the full fury. You'll show everyone both how much you know, and how much damage you can do. Whenever possible, you'll convince yourself that you're doing this for their own good. You escape your Darkest Self when you realize the loneliness of your choices.

Moves for all Legends

Choose three from this playbook and/or a subtype below:

Purpose

You have discovered your true purpose, the one big thing your life should revolve around. The first time in a scene that you spend a Fate Point in direct pursuit of that purpose, you receive a Fate Point.

Guardian

You can psychically prevent someone from crossing one specific physical boundary: for instance, doorways, property lines, roads or rivers. Roll with Will.

10-up: For the duration of the scene a magical barrier prevents their entry.

7-9: Choose one:

  • They cannot cross, but they gain a Fate point
  • They cannot cross, but you take a negative mental Aspect to keep the magic in place
  • They cross, but with the Aspect 'Hampered'

Suggestion

With a whisper you can plant a suggestion into someone's mind. Roll Will:

10+: They think the suggestion was their own idea

7-9: They hear it in your voice

Understanding

Once per scene, when you forgive or ignore a problem because you understand that character's faults and still accept them, take a Fate Point.

Sphinx

Moves only for Sphinxes:

Sphinx's Favor

-x Once per scene, you can channel mystic forces into riddles. You can give a solvable riddle as a payment or challenge. Each character that hears your riddle gains the 'Puzzled' Aspect until they solve it.

You gain +2 to all combat rolls, offensive or defensive, against characters with the 'Puzzled' Aspect.

Explosive Temper

If someone tries to shut you down, you can preemptively attack (verbally or physically) and take +1 to any rolls during the conflict.

Ancient Whispers

You can meditate, drawing visions of your past to answer your questions. Roll Will:

10+: You receive lucid visions, and you take +2 on your next roll to address them

7-9: You get confusing and alarming visions, but you get your answer nonetheless

Cultist

You owe your physical existence to the Leader of a cult. You can call upon the cult or its leader(s) to accomplish goals you shouldn't be seen doing. +2 to Contact rolls with them.

This is a subtype. Subtypes are mutually exclusive. You can choose a subtype without picking a move. When listing your playbook on your character sheet, list the subtype, if chosen.

Playing a Sphinx:

A thousand competing, arcane orders you'll be expected to fulfill, the fear of what they'll do if you fail and the dread of what they might ask you to do next. Being able to tell (and solve) riddles as a player is not a requirement but examples and ideas can be found online.

Fallen Angel

Moves only for Fallen Angels:

Trespass And Forgiveness

Once per scene you can choose 1:

  • Roll Rapport instead of Fight for the rest of the scene
  • Roll Provoke instead of Empathy for the rest of the scene

More Deserving

The first time in a scene someone else gets the praise that you deserve, add 1 Fate point.

Profane Powers

When not 'Drained' you are able to perform simple beneficial miracles: purifying poison, healing/curing minor ailments, teleportation, group flight and so on. Afterwards, you get the Aspect 'Drained' and can't perform another until you've had sufficient time to recover (can't be recovered with magic).

Smiting

When smiting the wicked, add +1 to your Physique or Fight rolls and add +1 to overlapping damage.

This is a subtype. Subtypes are mutually exclusive. You can choose a subtype without picking a move. When listing your playbook on your character sheet, list the subtype, if chosen.

Playing a Fallen Angel:

Not welcome in heaven right now. Locked out of its gates, and the lord isn't ready to take you back yet. He wants you to redeem yourself first. You've got all of his expectations and conditional acceptance on one hand, and all of your own might and desires on the other.

Kitsune

Moves only for Kitsune:

Dual Nature

-x You are a demon fox that can transform into a human at will. This transition is flawless, quick, and painless. You may have from 2-5 tails (9 at max lvl) depending on your age and power level. When you are in your fox form you are immune to fire and have rapid healing. Severe wounds or broken bones require extended rests in fox form to fully heal.

Pearl Of The Beholden

-x You have a singular magical pearl, the source of your powers. You can give it to the one you trust most, they gain the Beholden move 'Channel the Fox' and access to the "Beholden moves" for advancements. You may call out to your Beholden's pearl to: speak short messages, see through their eyes or will them to you, but your Beholden is aware of the use of power and the first time in a scene they come to your aid, they gain a Fate point.

Glamor

Your illusionary magic is strong enough to maintain a change to your appearance, even to mimic any person that you have encountered. This illusion usually has small flaws due to the nature of the kitsune's self-absorption. These flaws may be any small thing.

Aurora

You've mastered the ability to bend and shape your foxfire light, creating spectacular illusions of all kinds.

This is a subtype. Subtypes are mutually exclusive. You can choose a subtype without picking a move. When listing your playbook on your character sheet, list the subtype, if chosen.

Playing a Kitsune:

Beautiful, mischievous, and sometimes cruel. The Kitsune makes more enemies than friends. The biggest fault to the Kitsune is their lack of sympathy, they often are harsh and treat their Beholden as a slave or pet.

Moves for a Kitsune's Beholden

This one is available to any Beholden:

Channel The Fox

You have been gifted a Kitsune's Pearl and it cures you of your greatest flaw (Disease, genetics, age, physical disability, etc). Choose a skill. For as long as you are in possession of the pearl, gain a +1 in it.

Add this to your Darkest Self: Your love of the Pearl grows to near insane obsession, no one can come between you and it, or your Kitsune. Without the Pearl you cannot leave your Darkest Self.

These are available through advancements to any Beholden:

Refresh Loyalties

Sometimes you just have to remind your Kitsune why they chose you in the first place. Once per scene you may use one of the following abilities:

  • Spend a Fate point to help your Kitsune's roll
  • When in combat together, you may take an injury or Consequence intended for them
  • You can predict your Kitsune's needs. Add +1 on any rolls on them even when your Kitsune surprises you

Mine

You are special to your Kitsune and they are special to you, the most special. The first time in a scene your actions prevent your Kitsune from creating a new connection to another PC, take a Fate Point.

Requital

Any advance on your Kitsune is an attack against you. Defend yourself and take +1 to any conflict rolls to defend them.
