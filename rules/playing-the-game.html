Playing the Game

üé≠ Beginning Your Character's Story!

Once you get a Mod to check your sheet and add your Aspects as roles to your discord profile, you can go ahead and make a post in the character-profiles channel!

üìù Character Profile Format

Your character's profile post should include:

  • "Character's name (player discord name)"
  • Make your discord nickname like this too
  • A "human form" picture of your character, if applicable
  • Classification: what kind of creature they are (can be different than playbook)
  • Bio: very short, a sentence or two
  • Frequents: what part of town your character is likely to be found
  • Link: add your character sheet's "share" link from the google drive

üìå Important:

Hit enter to post it and then click on "create thread", simply title it the same way. In the thread you can put any further info about your character, use it to put info you want others to know, and use it as a way to track and remember important events/happenings and people, like a journal.

üèôÔ∏è Scene Locations

The Districts in town are massive areas with almost anything you can imagine contained within. Use the descriptions and ideas to help come up with specific areas within that district.

Begin each scene with -Scene Start- followed by an @ping for each other player in the scene (if any)

üéÆ Setting Up/Joining Scenes

Playing scenes is the actual game and when trying to think of what to write next ask yourself "what would my character do now?" and write them starting to try that. Always try to push onto the next interesting part of your character's story, if they aren't doing much or are waiting, use time jumps as needed.

üîç Finding Connections

Take a look through the Active Characters and see if there are any who your character could cross paths with. Characters with similar upbringing, schooling, jobs, hobbies or even secrets can be a way for your character to have noticed them somewhere.

üìö Using Backstory

Your character's backstory can be a great place to get ideas for their actions in the future. Why are they the way they are? What do they want to become, or do? What's the next thing they would try to do?

üåü Open Scenes

If your character isn't doing anything but loitering in a specific location you can temporarily post an OPEN SCENE tag.

How to Mark Open Scenes:

Simply post your solo-scene like normal (describing your characters actions like in a book) followed by a single post containing:

:OS:

Make sure to delete the tag when you continue the scene.

üí° Tip: You can search for open scenes by using the search function and looking for "os"

üè† Player Locations

Characters can have personal homes and/or businesses, but only if it makes story sense.

üè° Personal Homes

  • :PCH: Player Character Home
  • :NCH: Non-Player Character Home

üè¢ Businesses

  • :POL: Player Owned Location
  • :NOL: Non-Player Owned Location

üìç Setting Up Locations:

Simply decide which district you want your location to be in and make a post just like your character profile post. Add the proper tag to the start of your post.

Title the location; you can include a short general description and a single outside view of the place in the main post. Click on "create thread" - these threads are the "inside" of the locations. You will have conversations inside the location here (use the "pin" function to keep important info at hand like pictures and important characters).

üè∑Ô∏è Server Tags System

You can search the entire discord or specific channels and find important places, people and events throughout the city using tags.

üîç Searchable Tags

Use these tags to make your posts easily searchable too. Common tags include:

  • :OS: Open Scene
  • :PCH:/:NCH: Homes
  • :POL:/:NOL: Businesses
  • District names
  • Character names

üìù Best Practices

  • Always tag your scenes with appropriate tags
  • Remove :OS: tags when scene is no longer open
  • Use consistent tagging for easy searching
  • Pin important information in location threads
